One of the quiet joys of owning an EV that nobody mentions in the ads: you never have to make a special trip to fill up again. You plug in when you get home, and every morning the car is "full". For most people that is the single biggest lifestyle change — no more detours to the servo when you are already running late.

Home charging is also the cheapest way to keep the battery topped up. In fact, energy.gov.au reports that around 80 per cent of EV charging in Australia happens at home, usually overnight. Setting it up is straightforward, but there are a few decisions to make about hardware, installation and electricity pricing.

Level 1 vs Level 2 Charging

Every EV comes with a portable charger that plugs into a standard 240-volt household power point. This is Level 1 charging and delivers about 2.3 kW, adding roughly 10 to 15 kilometres of range per hour. For a daily commute of 40 to 60 kilometres, overnight Level 1 charging is sufficient.

Level 2 charging uses a dedicated wall box hardwired to your switchboard. On a single-phase connection (most Australian homes), a Level 2 charger delivers 7.2 kW — energy.gov.au notes dedicated home chargers run at 7 to 22 kW and can charge 3 to 10 times faster than a standard power point. On a three-phase connection, some chargers can deliver up to 22 kW. Level 2 charging adds 35 to 45 kilometres of range per hour on single-phase.

Choosing a Wall Box

Popular home charger brands in Australia include Tesla Wall Connector, Wallbox Pulsar Plus, Fronius Wattpilot, ABB Terra, and BYD's own charger for BYD vehicles. Key features to consider include:

Smart scheduling — charge only during off-peak hours to minimise electricity costs. Solar integration — some chargers communicate with your solar inverter to prioritise charging from excess solar generation. Load management — prevents your charger from overloading your home's electrical capacity. Cable length — choose a cable long enough to reach your car's charge port from where the charger is mounted.

Installation Costs

A typical Level 2 home charger installation in Australia costs between $1,500 and $3,000 including the unit and electrical work. The cost depends on the charger brand, the distance from your switchboard to the mounting location, whether your switchboard needs upgrading, and whether you need a new dedicated circuit.

Some states offer rebates. Victoria's Zero Emissions Vehicle subsidy and the ACT's Sustainable Household Scheme have included provisions for charger installation. Check your state government website for current offers.

Electricity Tariffs

The cost of charging at home depends entirely on your electricity plan. Key tariff types include:

Flat rate — a single price per kWh at all times. Time of use — different prices for peak, shoulder and off-peak periods. Controlled load — a separate meter for specific appliances that may include an EV charger. Rates vary by retailer, location and plan, so compare current offers rather than relying on a fixed national figure.

A smart charger set to charge only during off-peak hours can cut your charging costs by 40 to 60 per cent compared to charging at peak times. For a car using 16 kWh per 100 km driving 15,000 km per year, that is the difference between $600 and $360 annually.

Solar Charging

If you have rooftop solar, charging your EV from excess generation can substantially reduce the energy cost after the initial system investment. Many smart chargers can detect when your solar panels are exporting power to the grid and divert that energy to your car instead. Whether that is worthwhile depends on your feed-in tariff, electricity plan and the time your vehicle is at home.

Practical Tips

Install your charger near your car's charge port location to avoid running the cable around the vehicle. If you rent, talk to your landlord — many are open to charger installation as it adds property value. Ensure your home insurance covers the charger installation. Get quotes from at least two licensed electricians who have EV charger installation experience.

Home charging turns your garage or carport into your personal fuel station, available every night at a fraction of public charging prices.
